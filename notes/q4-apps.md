# Q4 — IoT Applications: Business + Government (Research Notes)

The exam asks for IoT in business AND government, with **3 SPECIFIC government examples**.

## Business-oriented IoT applications (sketch)
- Industrial IoT / Industry 4.0: predictive maintenance on factory machines, digital twins (Siemens, GE Predix), connected supply-chain.
- Logistics & fleet management: Wialon-platform fleets using Teltonika/Galileosky devices for cargo monitoring, eco-driving, fuel-theft detection.
- Smart agriculture: connected irrigation, soil-moisture sensors, livestock collars (John Deere, Climate FieldView).
- Connected retail: smart shelves, BLE beacons for in-store analytics, RFID inventory.
- Healthcare / insurance: wearables (Fitbit, Apple Watch) for UBI insurance and remote patient monitoring; FMB920 deployed for usage-based-insurance car premiums.

## Government-oriented IoT applications (need 3 specific)

### 1. Singapore — Smart Nation programme
- Source: tech.gov.sg (GovTech), smartnation.gov.sg
- A nation-scale IoT initiative led by GovTech and the Smart Nation Office.
- Concrete services: Smart Lamp Posts pilot (lampposts equipped with sensors for crowd density, environmental monitoring, security cameras), nationwide sensor network, MyInfo digital identity, contact-tracing TraceTogether tokens.
- Role of IoT: real-time situational awareness for the city-state government — public safety, transport, environment, public health.

### 2. Barcelona — Smart City Barcelona / Sentilo platform
- Source: datasmart.hks.harvard.edu (2016) + sdglocalaction.org
- 12 intervention areas, 22 programmes, 83 distinct projects across urban systems, on top of 500 km of municipal fibre.
- Concrete deployments:
  - 19 500 smart energy meters
  - Smart waste bins with fill-level sensors (route optimisation)
  - In-asphalt parking sensors + ApparkB app (4 000 parking permits/day via app)
  - 1 100+ LED lampposts that dim when streets are empty + air-quality sensors + free Wi-Fi (30 % lighting energy savings)
  - Park-irrigation sensors + remote electrovalves (25 % water savings, ~$555k/yr)
- Aggregated savings reported by city: $58M on water, $50M extra parking revenue, $37M on lighting, plus 47 000 jobs.
- Sentilo: open-source sensor-data-management platform built specifically for the city, now reusable by other governments.

### 3. Estonia — e-Estonia / X-Road data exchange layer
- Source: e-estonia.com
- X-Road is the secure data-exchange layer that interconnects Estonian state registers, banks, hospitals and IoT systems.
- Allied with: digital identity (eID, Mobile-ID), e-Residency, e-Health (sensor data from medical devices feeding the patient portal), e-Tax, i-Voting.
- Role of IoT: government IoT sensors (traffic, environment, smart grids) ride on X-Road to share data securely with citizens and other agencies — the protocol fabric of a digital state.
